Fire Detection (Random Forest vs SVM, who's better?!)

The Fire Detection project aims to develop an effective system for early fire detection, crucial for preventing disasters and safeguarding lives and property. This project compares the performance of two prominent machine learning algorithms: Random Forest and SVM. By analyzing environmental data and identifying fire indicators, the system strives to provide timely alerts to relevant authorities and individuals. The goal is to determine which algorithm performs best in accurately detecting fires, thereby enhancing safety measures and minimizing the damage caused by fire incidents. This project holds significant potential for improving fire detection technologies, thereby contributing to enhanced public safety and disaster management.
